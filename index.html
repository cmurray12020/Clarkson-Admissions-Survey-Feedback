üìã Executive Summary

  • Financial concerns dominate non-applicant decisions: 24% cited financial aid package/cost of attendance as the top reason for not applying, and 34% listed it as a contributing factor.
  • Awareness gap is critical: 35% of non-applicants felt they didn't know enough about Clarkson‚Äîthe most frequently cited contributing factor.
  • Location is a double-edged sword: Location (34%) and distance from home (29%) are major deterrents for non-applicants, yet enrolled families appreciate the rural setting for focused learning.
  • Word of mouth drives enrollment: 43% of enrolled families heard about Clarkson through personal connections‚Äîvs. only 12% of non-applicants.
  • Personal touch matters: Parents consistently praised individualized attention from admissions staff, coaches, and faculty as key factors in their decision.
  • Academic offerings are a strength: 65% of enrolled families chose Clarkson primarily for academic program offerings.

Survey 1: Non-Applicants Survey

This survey was sent to students who showed initial interest in Clarkson but ultimately did not apply during the 2025 admissions cycle. Understanding their decision drivers helps identify opportunities for improvement.

How Non-Applicants Heard About Clarkson

Discovery Channels for Non-Applicants (n=1,127)

Survey 2: First-Year Parents Survey

This survey captured insights from parents of students who enrolled at Clarkson for Fall 2025. Their perspectives reveal what's working well and where improvements can strengthen retention and satisfaction.

How Enrolled Families Discovered Clarkson

Discovery Channels for Enrolled Families (n=322)

‚úÖ Success Factor: Personal Connections Drive Enrollment

  • Word of mouth (43%) is the dominant channel for enrolled families
  • Legacy connections (26%) remain a strong pipeline
  • Leadership & Achievement Award (18%) and Athletic recruitment (15%) are effective targeted programs
  • Contrast with non-applicants: digital ads reach them first, but personal connections convert to enrollment
‚úÖ

Why Families Chose Clarkson

Reasons for Choosing Clarkson (Multi-Select, n=322)

‚úÖ Core Strengths to Emphasize

  • Academic Program Offerings (65%) ‚Äî the leading factor by a wide margin
  • Financial Aid Package/Cost of Attendance (55%) ‚Äî when students do apply, Clarkson's aid is competitive
  • Academic Reputation (52%) and Feeling/Fit (42%) round out the top factors
  • Career Placement (39%) is a significant draw‚Äîhighlight employment outcomes more prominently

Notable "Other" responses: Several parents mentioned specific differentiators including the Honors Program, direct acceptance into engineering specialties (vs. state schools requiring sophomore-year competition), The Clarkson School program, outdoor activities, and proximity to nature. One parent noted: "Our son had to be excited about Clarkson‚Äîwhich he was. The financial aid package made it as competitive if not even more affordable than NY state schools."

Improvement Opportunities from Parents

141 parents provided substantive feedback on what Clarkson could do better. The feedback clustered into several themes:

Communication & Information

  • "Better communication on what needed to be done once admitted. Felt like we had to scroll the Clarkson portal to figure out what we needed to do."
  • "I have a hard time with communication. I can't find anything and do not receive any communication about financial aid or parents weekend."
  • "Included me and not just my ex-husband" ‚Äî ensuring both parents receive communications

Financial Aid Process

  • "Finances are a huge decision maker. Maybe having financial aid representatives more present and available."
  • "Explain that my child would owe several thousand dollars up front when attending even after financial aid and student loans."
  • "Replied to my daughter's email about help with a little more financial aid‚Ä¶.she received no response."

What's Working Well

  • "Brian Grant personally wrote a little note to our son after the visit and it wasn't vague‚Äîit was very personalized."
  • "I was the most impressed by Clarkson in the admissions process. There is nothing that I would change."
  • "Very fast as we applied very late."
  • "We were very pleased with our experience, particularly the help and communication with Adam Jaquish, we were in frequent communication during the process."

Additional Feedback (from general comments)

  • "The communication around events and information is lacking, especially for first year students and parents. Understanding what to expect, where to get information and how to get information would be very helpful."

üéØ Top Action Items from Parent Feedback

  • Streamline post-acceptance checklist ‚Äî Create a clear, step-by-step guide for admitted students
  • Improve parent communication portal ‚Äî Ensure both parents receive updates; simplify navigation
  • Financial aid responsiveness ‚Äî Ensure all inquiries receive timely responses
  • Update website content ‚Äî Several mentions of outdated information
