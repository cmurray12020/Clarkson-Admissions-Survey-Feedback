Executive Summary

The Gold Standard of Campus Visits

A Day as a Knight continues to deliver as one of Clarkson's most effective yield tools. The event moves the needle: nearly half of respondents reported increased interest in Clarkson after attending, and zero came away "not very interested."

Open-Ended Feedback

In Their Own Words

When asked what was most useful, one theme dominated every other: the student shadowing experience. Respondents repeatedly pointed to the authenticity of spending real time with a current student in real classes as the moment that sealed the deal.

Most-Cited Themes — "Most Useful Component"
🎓

Shadowing & Class Attendance

The clear winner. Respondents valued attending real lectures, seeing teaching styles firsthand and experiencing the rhythm of a genuine college day with a current student as guide.

🗣️

Faculty Interaction at Lunch

Parents called this out repeatedly. One-on-one conversations with professors over lunch were described as "intimate," "personal" and a standout differentiator from other schools' visit days.

🏗️

Areas for Improvement

Where We Can Sharpen the Sword

Most improvement feedback was positive in disguise ("Nothing, it was great!"). The constructive themes that did emerge clustered around shadow match quality and day-of logistics.

Recurring Improvement Themes
🔗

Shadow Match Quality

The most frequent concern. Some students were paired with hosts who had no classes that day, were in a different major or didn't show campus life beyond lectures. Multiple respondents asked for better alignment by interest and personality, plus hosts who live on campus.

🅿️

Parking & Wayfinding

The ERC lot filled up early with no overflow direction. Families parked as far as Cheel Arena. Requests for signage, a parking attendant and a printed campus map came up repeatedly.

📧

Pre-Event Communication

Some found emails "aggressive" or unclear on logistics. Others wanted shadow student schedules shared ahead of time and better check-in coordination upon arrival.

🍽️

Food & Dietary Options

Limited options for GF, DF and other restrictions. General sentiment that food variety at the parent lunch could be improved, with one respondent specifically noting Clarkson's dining needs to compete with peer institutions.

📋

Space & Crowding

Parent rooms were crowded and seating was tight. SPEED lab tours had 30+ parents in a single group. Several respondents asked for smaller group sizes, more breaks and a larger home base room.

🎙️

Panel & Representation

Microphone issues made the panel hard to hear from the back. Parents also requested broader major representation on the student panel, particularly Health Sciences.

Recommendations

Forging a Stronger Knight's Tale

Based on the 2026 data, here are six recommendations to build on what's already working and address the gaps.

1. Tighten Shadow Matching

Build a structured matching form that captures major, interests and personality. Require hosts to have at least one class on event day, verify schedules in advance and ensure hosts live on campus when possible. Consider a brief "host training" checklist covering dorms, dining and campus life beyond the classroom.

2. Share Itineraries Earlier

Send a personalized itinerary (including the shadow student's name and class schedule) 48 hours before the event. Include a campus map with parking alternatives and event locations clearly marked. Let shadow pairs connect by email beforehand.

3. Solve Parking Proactively

Station a parking attendant at the ERC lot to redirect overflow. Pre-designate a secondary lot with signage. Include parking info and a map prominently in pre-event communications.

4. Break Up Large Groups

Cap SPEED lab tours at 10–12 people per group. Stagger parent sessions to reduce room crowding, add short breaks between panels and consider a larger home base space.

5. Expand Food & Dietary Options

Add clearly labeled dietary options (GF, DF, vegan) at the parent lunch. Survey dietary needs during registration so the catering order reflects actual demand rather than best guesses.

6. Refresh the Family Mingle

This scored lowest among all components with 31% neutral. Add light icebreaker prompts, conversation-starter cards or optional guided discussion topics. Give parents a reason to engage with each other beyond proximity.

Conclusion

The Armor Shines Bright

A Day as a Knight is doing exactly what it was designed to do: convert accepted students into committed Golden Knights. With a 94% worthwhile rating, 49% of attendees reporting increased interest and open-ended feedback that reads like a love letter to student shadowing, the event is a proven yield asset. The improvement areas are logistical rather than structural, which is the best kind of problem to have. Tighten the matching process, smooth out the parking and communications, give the Family Mingle some life, and this event will continue to be worth its weight in gold.
